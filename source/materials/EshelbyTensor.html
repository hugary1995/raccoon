Eshelby Tensor

Computes the Eshelby tensor as a function of strain energy density and the first Piola-Kirchhoff stress

Description

This model computes the Eshelby energy-momentum tensor (Eshelby, 1999), used in fracture integral calculations: where W is the strain energy density in the original configuration, is the identity matrix, is the displacement gradient, and is the first Piola-Kirchhoff stress tensor.

It is necessary to include this material within the input file when computing fracture integrals.
